Science-Backed
Evidence-Based Fitness Tips

No pseudoscience. Every tip references peer-reviewed research or verified clinical data.

01

Protein at Every Meal

Aim for 0.4 g of protein per kg of bodyweight per meal across 4 meals. Norton & Layman (2011) showed that distributing 20–40 g of high-quality protein across meals maximises 24-hour muscle protein synthesis compared to front-loading protein at one meal.

Norton & Layman, Journal of Nutrition, 2011
02

Carbohydrates Are Not the Enemy

Carbohydrates are the body's primary fuel substrate. Focus on low-GI sources — oats (GI ~55), sweet potato (GI ~50), and legumes (GI ~28–40) — timed around training to replenish muscle glycogen without triggering excess fat storage.

Thomas et al., Journal of the Academy of Nutrition & Dietetics, 2016
03

Track for 14 Days

A 2019 study in Obesity found that participants who logged food for just 14–28 days developed lasting portion-size awareness, consuming an average of 224 fewer calories daily at 6-month follow-up — without continuing to track. Short-term precision creates long-term habits.

Harvey et al., Obesity, 2019
04

Sleep Is a Nutritional Lever

Sleep deprivation (<6 hrs) elevates ghrelin by ~28% and suppresses leptin by ~18% (Spiegel et al., 2004), increasing appetite — particularly for calorie-dense foods. Prioritising 7–9 hours is one of the most impactful dietary interventions with no calorie cost.

Spiegel et al., PLOS Medicine, 2004
05

Pre-Meal Water Reduces Intake

Drinking 500 ml of water 30 minutes before each meal led to 44% greater weight loss over 12 weeks in a randomised controlled trial by Davy et al. (2008). Water occupies gastric volume and slows gastric emptying, reducing ad-libitum intake by ~13% per meal.

Davy et al., Obesity, 2008
06

Progressive Overload Is Non-Negotiable

Muscle growth requires progressive mechanical tension. Adding 2.5 kg to compound lifts every 1–2 weeks during beginner–intermediate phases produces measurable hypertrophy. Without increasing load or volume over time, muscle protein synthesis returns to baseline within 6 weeks.

Schoenfeld, Journal of Strength & Conditioning Research, 2010
07

Weekly Weigh-In Beats Daily

Bodyweight fluctuates 1–3 kg within a single day due to hydration, glycogen, and gut content. Weigh at the same time each morning once per week and compute a rolling 4-week average. Daily weighing increases anxiety without improving outcomes.

Pacanowski et al., Journal of Behavioral Medicine, 2015
08

NEAT: The Overlooked Calorie Burn

Non-Exercise Activity Thermogenesis (NEAT) — all movement outside formal exercise — accounts for 15–50% of total daily energy expenditure. Sedentary individuals burn 350–700 fewer calories daily than active peers. 8,000–10,000 steps adds an estimated 300–500 kcal/day.

Levine et al., Science, 2005
09

Batch Cooking Reduces Calories

A study of 40,000 adults (Ducrot et al., 2017) found that individuals who batch-cooked meals 3+ times per week consumed 137 fewer daily calories, had higher diet quality scores, and were 24% less likely to be overweight. Preparation removes the high-calorie default of convenience food.

Ducrot et al., International Journal of Behavioral Nutrition, 2017
